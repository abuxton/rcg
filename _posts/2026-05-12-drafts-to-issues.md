After revisiting my older post,
["Things on my mind"]({{ '/2024/05/10/things-on-my-mind.html' | relative_url }}),
I wanted to stop letting ideas sit quietly in `_drafts/` and start tracking them in
the open.

I've now got a set of GitHub issues to use as a working queue, including:

- [Draft Review: apeiron](https://github.com/abuxton/rcg/issues/33)
- [Draft Review: Bad Company LARP](https://github.com/abuxton/rcg/issues/34)
- [Draft Review: GPS tracking players](https://github.com/abuxton/rcg/issues/35)
- [Draft Review: Strontium Dog LARP](https://github.com/abuxton/rcg/issues/36)
- [Draft Review: Invite only events](https://github.com/abuxton/rcg/issues/37)
- [Draft Review: Player identitys rfid](https://github.com/abuxton/rcg/issues/38)

That gives me a clearer backlog and a better prompt to either write or retire each
idea.

I also removed my old Facebook draft. Facebook can still be a good place for
organising tabletop games, but I no longer use it as a platform myself, so writing
about it now feels awkward and out of date for me.
